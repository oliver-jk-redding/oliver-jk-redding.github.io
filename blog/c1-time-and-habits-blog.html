Time and Habits

09 December 2015

Which timebox and habits techniques did I try?

I tried to timebox the time I spent reading the reflection resources and the Time Management resources. I am currently putting into practice the Pomodoro technique, by trying to stay focused and productive for 25 minute blocks. I resisted the temtation to check email and facebook. I am hoping by these practices to get closer to achieving a flow state.

What Did I Find Interesting?

I found the theory around the state of flow concept very interesting. I have experienced this before, but it is now my intention to become more focused and achieve this on a more regular basis. I thrive in finding my state of flow. It is something I was rarely able to achieve as a teacher as teaching is full of interruptions. I had not even realised this was frustrating me. I realise now it was one of the main motivations for me wanting to get out of teaching. I am looking forward to entering a job space where hopefully I can achieve a state of flow more regularly. The techniques around timeboxing and time management were also interesting. I am hoping to put some of these into practice to increase my productivity. Time is very precious to me and I look forward to saving more of it in future!

What worked for me?

Timeboxing definitely helped me focus. Knowing I am on the clock, helped me to cut out time-wasting distractions. It helped me to focus on my outcomes. One change I noticed was that I was able to skim read more effectively. Usually, when I attempt to skim read, I either get bogged down in text, or skim through without taking in the content. Using techniques like timeboxing I was able to enhance my skim reading abilities by reading quickly and taking in content at the same time.

Which will I experiment further with?

I am keen to experiment further with the timeboxing technique. I am especially interested in the pamodoro technique. I want to use it to estimate time to finish tasks and try to keep to that estimation. I want to be more goal-oriented in my work. I want to set goals each day and with each task, and try to achieve those. Getting the most out of time is vital to success.
