Meditation Process

31 December 2015

How did the 'process over product' concept affect the way I tackled the site redesign and rebuild?

To a limited extent. I think I focused too much on creating an awesome looking website and probably spent too much time perfecting my features. This is something I still need to work on. I did however not put in all the features I wanted, and I could easily have kept working on it to make it even better, but I forced myself to stop once I reached a point with which I was satisfied.

What did I think about meditation before reading chapter 2?

I thought it was a bit of a pointless waste of time. I did not think I could receive relevant benefits from it.

What new things have I learned about meditation?

I learned that meditation has tangible measurable benefits such as increased concentration, better meta attention, thicker neocortex, and social improvements. I learned that we can increase our meditative skills through regular meditation and that this is like exercising the mind.

Did any of the suggested meditation techniques stand out to me?

I liked how it said not to be hard on oneself if one's attention wandered, but instead to gently bring the attention back. I like how it said that if one does this enough times, one can eventually develop one's attention skills to the point one can achieve continuous focus at will.

Other musings

The meditation techniques work well given a normal day, but various pressures and stresses can get in the way, especially if these stresses are urgent and/or traumatic. I wonder how meditation can help us deal with major feelings of grief or hurt or anxiety. Also does meditation work for everyone equally? Science shows that not everyone responds to exercise in the same way. Perhaps some people find meditation easier than others. For me, I find it a challenge, but perhaps this is because I have not done it much.
